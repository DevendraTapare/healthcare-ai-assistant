WHAT IS HIPAA AND WHY IT MATTERS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) is a United
States federal law that establishes national standards for the protection of
sensitive patient health information. Although Mindbowser Health Clinic
operates primarily in India, we voluntarily adopt HIPAA standards as part of
our commitment to international best practices in patient data privacy and
security, and to support patients whose care involves US-based insurers,
employers, or healthcare networks.

Protected Health Information (PHI) refers to any individually identifiable
health information — including name, date of birth, address, diagnosis, test
results, treatment history, and insurance details — that is created, received,
stored, or transmitted by the clinic in any form (paper, electronic, or oral).

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE MINIMUM NECESSARY STANDARD
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Under the HIPAA Minimum Necessary Standard, Mindbowser Health Clinic limits
access to PHI to the minimum information needed to accomplish the intended
purpose. Staff may only access PHI that is directly relevant to their job
function. For example, a billing specialist may access diagnosis codes for
claims processing but does not have access to detailed clinical notes.

This standard applies to all disclosures of PHI except those made directly
to the patient themselves, those required for treatment by a treating provider,
or those mandated by law or court order.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The clinic may use and disclose PHI without patient authorisation for the
following purposes (the "TPO" exceptions):

1. Treatment: Sharing information among treating physicians, nurses, specialists,
   laboratories, and other care providers involved in the patient's direct care.

2. Payment: Disclosing information to insurance companies, government health
   schemes (e.g., AB-PMJAY), and billing agencies to process claims and obtain
   reimbursement.

3. Healthcare Operations: Internal quality assurance, staff training, compliance
   audits, accreditation reviews, and de-identified data analytics.

For any use beyond TPO — such as marketing, research, or sharing with employers
— the clinic must obtain explicit written authorisation from the patient.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PATIENT RIGHTS UNDER HIPAA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Patients at Mindbowser Health Clinic have the following rights regarding their PHI:

Right of Access: Patients may request a copy of their medical records, including
discharge summaries, lab results, imaging reports, and prescription history.
Requests are fulfilled within 30 days. A nominal fee (not to exceed ₹250 per
request) may apply for reproduction costs.

Right to Amend: If a patient believes their health record contains an error or
omission, they may request an amendment. The clinic will review and respond
within 60 days. If the amendment is denied, the patient may submit a written
statement of disagreement.

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures: Patients may request a list of all
non-routine disclosures of their PHI made in the past six years, excluding
TPO disclosures and direct care communications.

Right to Request Restrictions: Patients may request that the clinic limit how
their PHI is used or disclosed. The clinic will honour requests to restrict
disclosure to a health plan when the patient pays for the service in full
out of pocket.

Right to Confidential Communications: Patients may request that communications
be sent via a specific method or to an alternate address (e.g., work email
instead of home address) if disclosure could endanger them.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ELECTRONIC PHI (ePHI) SAFEGUARDS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All electronic PHI is protected by administrative, physical, and technical
safeguards under the HIPAA Security Rule:

- Access controls: Role-based access with unique user IDs and strong passwords.
- Audit controls: All access to the Electronic Health Record (EHR) system is
  logged with timestamp, user ID, and records accessed.
- Encryption: All ePHI in transit uses TLS 1.2 or higher; stored data is
  encrypted at rest using AES-256.
- Automatic logoff: EHR workstations time out after 10 minutes of inactivity.
- Breach notification: In the event of a PHI breach, affected patients are
  notified within 60 days in accordance with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.
