MEDICATION REFILL POLICY
Mindbowser Health Clinic — Pharmacy and Outpatient Services
Document Version: 2.5 | Effective Date: March 2024

This policy outlines the procedures and requirements for requesting prescription
medication refills at Mindbowser Health Clinic. All refill requests are subject
to physician review and applicable legal regulations for drug dispensing.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GENERAL REFILL GUIDELINES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Prescription medication refills are not automatic. Each refill request must be
reviewed and approved by the prescribing physician or their designated covering
provider before the pharmacy can dispense the medication.

Patients are strongly advised to submit refill requests at least 5 business
days before their current medication supply runs out. Requests submitted with
fewer than 5 days of medication remaining may experience delays, particularly
for medications requiring prior authorisation from insurance providers.

Controlled substances (Schedule H, H1, and X medications under the Drugs and
Cosmetics Act, including opioids, benzodiazepines, and stimulants) require an
in-person consultation before each refill and cannot be processed via phone,
portal message, or email alone.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW TO REQUEST A REFILL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Patient Portal (Preferred): Log in at portal.mbhealthclinic.com, go to
   "My Prescriptions," select the medication you need refilled, and click
   "Request Refill." Include the name of the medication, dosage, and your
   preferred pharmacy for pickup. You will receive a portal notification when
   the request has been reviewed (typically within 2 business days).

2. Phone: Call the Pharmacy Line at +91-20-6789-0400, Monday to Friday,
   9 AM to 5 PM IST. Have your patient ID, current prescription number,
   medication name, and dosage ready when you call.

3. In-Person: Visit the Outpatient Pharmacy on the ground floor. Present
   your current prescription label or pharmacy receipt.

4. Authorised Representative: A family member or caregiver may request a
   refill on your behalf, provided they present a signed written authorisation
   from the patient and a copy of the patient's photo ID.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEDICATIONS NOT ELIGIBLE FOR REFILL WITHOUT CONSULTATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The following categories of medications require a new in-person or telehealth
consultation before a refill can be authorised:

- All Schedule H1 (controlled) medications
- Antibiotics (refills indicate possible treatment failure — reassessment needed)
- Immunosuppressants and biologics (require current lab monitoring values)
- Anticoagulants such as warfarin (requires current INR result)
- Insulin and other injectable diabetes medications (annual review required)
- Medications that have not been refilled in more than 12 months

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MAXIMUM REFILL QUANTITIES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

For most chronic condition medications (hypertension, diabetes, thyroid),
the clinic authorises up to a 90-day supply per refill, subject to insurance
coverage limits. Acute condition medications (short-term antibiotics,
post-surgical pain medications) are limited to a single fill with no refills
unless re-evaluated. Emergency supply: A 3-day emergency supply may be
dispensed by the pharmacist at their discretion for critical chronic medications
(e.g., antihypertensives, anticoagulants) when a refill request is pending.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
INSURANCE AND REFILL TIMING
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Many insurance plans enforce a "too soon to fill" restriction, which prevents
a refill from being dispensed before a minimum number of days have elapsed
since the last fill (typically when 75% of the previous supply should have
been consumed). If your refill is rejected by the pharmacy due to this
restriction, contact our billing team at +91-20-6789-0200 to explore options
including appeal, generic substitution, or a physician override request.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEDICATION CHANGES AND SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If your prescribed medication is unavailable at our pharmacy or is out of
stock, our pharmacist will contact your prescribing physician to discuss a
therapeutically equivalent alternative. Substitutions are made only with
physician approval. Patients will be notified of any substitution before
dispensing and have the right to decline the substitution.

Generic substitution is performed by default when a generic equivalent is
available unless the physician has specified "brand medically necessary" on
the prescription. Generic medications are bioequivalent to brand-name versions
and meet the same quality standards.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DISPOSAL OF UNUSED MEDICATIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Do not flush unused medications down the drain or throw them in household
waste. Mindbowser Health Clinic operates a medication disposal drop-box
located near the outpatient pharmacy entrance. All disposed medications are
collected by a licensed medical waste handler in accordance with the
Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016.
