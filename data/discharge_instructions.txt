PATIENT DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS
Mindbowser Health Clinic — Patient Care Services
Document Version: 3.2 | Effective Date: January 2024

IMPORTANT: Please read these instructions carefully before leaving the facility.
Keep this document for your reference during your recovery period.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GENERAL CARE AFTER DISCHARGE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Following your discharge, it is essential to rest adequately and allow your body
to heal. Most patients require a minimum of 48 to 72 hours of reduced activity
after a standard inpatient procedure. Avoid strenuous physical activities,
heavy lifting (anything above 5 kg), or vigorous exercise for at least two weeks
unless your physician has specifically cleared you for such activities.

Ensure your living environment is safe and accessible. If you live alone,
arrange for a responsible adult to stay with you for the first 24 hours
post-discharge. This is especially important if you have received general
anaesthesia, sedatives, or narcotic pain medications, as these can impair
judgment and coordination.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WOUND CARE AND HYGIENE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Keep all wound sites clean and dry for a minimum of 48 hours. Change dressings
as instructed by your nurse before discharge. Inspect the wound daily for signs
of infection including increased redness, swelling, warmth, discharge of pus,
or a wound that reopens. Report any of these signs to your care team immediately.
Do not submerge wounds in water (baths, pools, or open water bodies) until
cleared by your physician. Showers are generally acceptable after 48 hours
unless otherwise instructed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEDICATIONS AFTER DISCHARGE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Take all prescribed medications exactly as directed on your discharge prescription.
Do not skip doses or discontinue antibiotics early, even if you feel better.
If you experience side effects such as severe rash, difficulty breathing, or
swelling of the face or throat, stop the medication and seek emergency care
immediately. Store medications as directed — some require refrigeration.
Do not share your medications with anyone else.

Over-the-counter pain relief such as paracetamol (acetaminophen) may be used
for mild discomfort unless contraindicated by your physician. Avoid ibuprofen
or other NSAIDs without physician approval if you have kidney concerns, a
history of peptic ulcers, or are on blood thinners.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DIET AND NUTRITION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Resume a light, easily digestible diet for the first 24 hours. Prefer soups,
khichdi, toast, bananas, and boiled vegetables. Stay well-hydrated by drinking
at least 8 glasses of water per day unless your physician has advised fluid
restriction. Avoid alcohol entirely for at least 48 hours after discharge,
and longer if you are on any prescription medications.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FOLLOW-UP APPOINTMENT
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A follow-up appointment has been scheduled or is required within 7 to 14 days
of discharge depending on your procedure. Please confirm this appointment at
the front desk or via the Patient Portal at portal.mbhealthclinic.com before
you leave the facility. Missing your follow-up visit may delay recovery and
could result in undetected complications.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY CARE IMMEDIATELY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Go to the nearest Emergency Department or call 112 if you experience any of
the following warning signs:

- Fever above 38.5°C (101.3°F) that does not respond to paracetamol
- Chest pain, pressure, or tightness
- Sudden shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
- Severe or rapidly worsening pain not controlled by prescribed medication
- Fainting, loss of consciousness, or confusion
- Calf pain, swelling, or redness (possible deep vein thrombosis)
- Uncontrolled bleeding from the wound site
- Sudden numbness or weakness in the arms or legs
