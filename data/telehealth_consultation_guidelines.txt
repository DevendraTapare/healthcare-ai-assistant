TELEHEALTH CONSULTATION GUIDELINES
Mindbowser Health Clinic — Digital Health Services
Document Version: 1.6 | Effective Date: April 2024

These guidelines cover the eligibility criteria, technical requirements,
conduct, limitations, and privacy protections applicable to all telehealth
(virtual) consultations offered by Mindbowser Health Clinic.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT IS A TELEHEALTH CONSULTATION?
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A telehealth consultation is a scheduled, live, two-way audio-video interaction
between a patient and a licensed Mindbowser Health Clinic physician or specialist
conducted via the clinic's secure telehealth platform. Telehealth is not a
messaging service or chatbot — a qualified doctor participates in real time and
clinical decisions are made during the session.

Telehealth consultations are appropriate for:
- Follow-up visits for stable, established conditions
- Review of test results, imaging reports, or specialist findings
- Management of chronic conditions (hypertension, diabetes, thyroid, asthma)
- Mental health consultations (psychiatry, counselling)
- Minor acute conditions (mild respiratory symptoms, rash review, UTI)
- Prescription renewals that do not require physical examination
- Second opinion consultations
- Post-discharge check-ins within the first 30 days after hospitalisation

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CONDITIONS NOT SUITABLE FOR TELEHEALTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Telehealth is not appropriate for emergencies or conditions requiring physical
examination or hands-on intervention. Patients should present in person or
go to the Emergency Department for:

- Chest pain, suspected heart attack, or stroke symptoms
- Severe abdominal pain or suspected appendicitis
- High fever with altered mental status
- Fractures, deep lacerations, or serious injuries
- First-time assessment of a new, undiagnosed complex symptom
- Conditions requiring blood draws, imaging, or on-site diagnostic testing
- Procedures such as injections, dressing changes, or minor surgery
- Paediatric emergencies or infants under 3 months with any illness

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To participate in a telehealth session, patients require:

- A smartphone, tablet, or computer with a working camera and microphone
- A stable internet connection (minimum 5 Mbps recommended for HD video)
- The Mindbowser Health Clinic Telehealth App (available on iOS App Store and
  Google Play) or access via the web portal at telehealth.mbhealthclinic.com
- A quiet, well-lit, private location to protect confidentiality
- The ability to view the full body or affected area if relevant (e.g., for
  dermatology consultations, position your camera to show the affected skin area)

A technical support team is available at +91-20-6789-0500 to assist patients
who encounter difficulties connecting to their telehealth appointment.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW TO BOOK A TELEHEALTH CONSULTATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Log in to the Patient Portal at portal.mbhealthclinic.com.
2. Select "Book Appointment" and choose "Telehealth / Virtual Visit."
3. Select your preferred department and physician.
4. Choose a date and time slot. Telehealth slots are marked with a video icon.
5. Complete your reason-for-visit questionnaire (required for the doctor's
   pre-session review).
6. You will receive a confirmation with a secure session link 30 minutes before
   your appointment. Do not share this link with anyone.

Telehealth consultations may also be booked by calling the scheduling desk at
+91-20-6789-0000.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DURING THE CONSULTATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Join the session 5 minutes before your scheduled time using the link in your
confirmation message. Your physician will admit you from a virtual waiting room.
Please have the following ready at the start of the call:

- Your current list of medications and dosages
- Any recent test results or reports relevant to the consultation
- A list of your symptoms, including duration and severity
- Any questions you wish to raise with the physician

The consultation is conducted by a licensed physician and follows the same
standard of care as an in-person visit. The physician may escalate you to
an in-person appointment if clinical assessment requires physical examination.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRESCRIPTIONS AND REFERRALS AFTER TELEHEALTH
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Prescriptions issued during a telehealth consultation are digitally signed and
transmitted directly to your registered pharmacy or made available for download
via the Patient Portal within 30 minutes of the session ending. Controlled
substances (Schedule H1 and X) cannot be prescribed via telehealth — an
in-person consultation is required.

Referral letters to specialists generated during a telehealth visit are
uploaded to your Patient Portal within 24 hours and can be shared directly
with the specialist's office through the portal.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRIVACY AND CONFIDENTIALITY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All telehealth sessions are conducted over an end-to-end encrypted connection.
Sessions are never recorded without explicit written consent from the patient.
Video and audio data are not stored beyond the duration of the call. Session
notes are recorded in the patient's EHR by the physician and are protected
under the same PHI safeguards as in-person consultation records.

Patients must ensure they are in a private location during the consultation.
The clinic is not responsible for breaches of confidentiality caused by the
patient conducting the session in a non-private environment.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FEES AND INSURANCE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Telehealth consultation fees are equivalent to the corresponding in-person
consultation fee for the same department. Most major health insurance plans
cover telehealth at the same co-pay rate as in-person visits. Patients should
verify telehealth coverage with their insurer before booking. Uninsured patients
may pay via the Patient Portal using UPI, debit/credit card, or net banking.
